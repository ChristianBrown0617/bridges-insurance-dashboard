Coverage overview
What to talk about first

Health Insurance

Highest priority
  • Shields from medical bills that often lead to bankruptcy.
  • Look for: in-network care, out-of-pocket max, subsidies/Medicaid.
  • Okay to choose a higher deductible if it’s all you can afford.

Renters / Homeowners

Shelter & belongings
  • Renters: covers your stuff + liability (often $10–$20/mo).
  • Homeowners: protects the house + liability; required by lenders.
  • Remind: landlord’s policy does not cover tenants’ belongings.

Auto Insurance

Legally required (if you drive)
  • Start above state minimum liability if possible.
  • Raise deductibles & bundle to lower cost.
  • Driving without insurance = huge legal & financial risk.

Life Insurance

If someone relies on your income
  • Term life: most coverage for lowest cost.
  • Rule of thumb: ~5–10× annual income (adjust to family needs).
  • Work policies alone often aren’t enough.

Disability Insurance

Protect your paycheck
  • More likely to use than life insurance.
  • Check employer first; consider LTD if affordable.
  • Covers part of income if illness/injury stops work.

Umbrella Insurance

Extra liability protection
  • Adds $1M+ coverage above auto/home for low monthly cost.
  • Useful once clients own a home, have savings, or teen drivers.
