Insurance Protection Overview

Start with the protections that prevent the biggest financial disasters. Then layer on what fits the household’s goals and budget.

Essential Coverage

  • Health: shields from major medical bills.
  • Auto: required if you drive; protects from lawsuits.
  • Home/Renters: protects belongings & liability.

Important Coverage

  • Life: for anyone with dependents.
  • Disability: protects your paycheck.

Optional (After Basics)

  • Umbrella: extra liability once you have assets.
  • Special coverages (flood, etc.) for specific risks.

Key Insurance Terms

Understanding these makes every decision easier. Use them with real examples.

Premium

The amount you pay (usually monthly) to keep coverage active.
You pay this whether or not you file a claim.

Deductible

What you pay out of pocket on a covered claim before insurance pays.
Example: $1,000 deductible → you pay first $1,000.

Co-pay

A fixed fee for specific services, like a $20 doctor visit.

Coinsurance

After the deductible, you pay a percentage, the plan pays the rest (e.g., 20% you / 80% plan).

Out-of-Pocket Maximum

The most you'll pay for covered services in a year. After that, the plan pays 100% of covered costs.

Claim

A request for your insurer to pay for a covered loss or service.

Health Insurance

Highest priority for most households. Focus on avoiding catastrophic medical debt.

Where Coverage Comes From

  • Employer plans
  • Healthcare.gov / ACA Marketplace
  • Medicaid / CHIP (income-based)
  • Medicare (65+ or certain disabilities)
Check for subsidies or Medicaid first.

How to Choose

  • Compare total cost: premium + deductible + OOP max.
  • Use in-network providers.
  • High deductible can work if you have savings.

Auto Insurance

Required if you drive. Protects you from lawsuits, injuries, and damage.

Key Coverages

  • Liability (required): injuries & damage you cause.
  • Collision: damage to your car in a crash.
  • Comprehensive: theft, fire, weather, etc.
  • Uninsured/Underinsured: when others lack coverage.

Keeping It Affordable

  • Maintain clean driving record.
  • Raise deductibles if you can cover them.
  • Bundle with home/renters.
  • Ask about discounts (safe driver, student).
Never drive uninsured.

Homeowners & Renters Insurance

Protects your home or belongings and your liability if someone is hurt on your property.

Homeowners

  • Dwelling at replacement cost.
  • Personal property (your stuff).
  • Liability + medical payments.
  • Loss of use if you can’t live there.
Often required by lenders.

Renters

  • Personal property coverage.
  • Liability protection.
  • Very low cost ($10–$20/month typical).
Landlord’s policy ≠ your stuff.

Life Insurance

For anyone whose income supports others. Term life is usually best value.

Basics

  • Term life 10–30 years fits most needs.
  • Common rule: ~5–10× annual income.
  • Employer coverage alone is often too small.

Who Needs It Most

  • Parents with kids.
  • Partners relying on shared income.
Focus on affordable term, not cash-value extras.

Disability Insurance

Protects your paycheck if illness or injury stops you from working. Statistically more likely than an early death.

What It Does

  • Replaces part of your income (often 50–70%).
  • Short-term and long-term options.

Where to Start

  • Check employer benefits.
  • Explore individual policies if needed & affordable.
Key for households living paycheck to paycheck.

Umbrella Insurance

Extra liability coverage above auto & home—cheap way to protect against big lawsuits.

How It Works

  • Kicks in after regular policy limits are hit.
  • Common starting limit: $1 million.

Who Should Consider

  • Homeowners with savings.
  • Teen drivers, rentals, higher visibility, etc.
Add after essentials are in place.

Ways to Save Without Dangerous Gaps

Help clients lower premiums while keeping the protections that matter.

Smart Strategies

  • Bundle auto + home/renters.
  • Raise deductibles if you have an emergency fund.
  • Ask for all discounts (safe driver, student, devices).

Check Programs

  • Marketplace subsidies.
  • Medicaid / CHIP eligibility.
  • Employer contributions.
